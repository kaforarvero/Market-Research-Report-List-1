<p><p>The MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) mirrors market is segmented into 1D and 2D types. 1D MEMS mirrors are designed to tilt along a single axis, primarily used in applications like optical switches and barcode scanning. In contrast, 2D MEMS mirrors can tilt along two axes, enabling more complex functionalities such as projecting images and advanced optical communications. Both types leverage micromachining technology, catering to diverse applications in telecommunications, automotive, and consumer electronics, driven by demand for miniaturization and precision.</p></p>
