<p><p>The nonwovens for filtration market comprises various production methods tailored for specific applications. Airlaid processes produce absorbent materials, suitable for air filtration. Wetlaid technology creates fine, uniform fabrics ideal for liquid filtration. Spunlaid involves the extrusion of fibers for strong, lightweight structures, while meltblown offers fine fibers for high-efficiency filtration. Needlepunching entangles fibers for durability, hydro-entanglement uses water jets for enhanced density, and thermobonded ensures stability through heat for robust filtration solutions across industries.</p></p>
