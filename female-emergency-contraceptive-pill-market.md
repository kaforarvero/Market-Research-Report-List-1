<p><p>The female emergency contraceptive pill market consists primarily of two main types: Levonorgestrel and Ulipristal Acetate. Levonorgestrel is a progestin that prevents ovulation when taken within 72 hours after unprotected intercourse, while Ulipristal Acetate is a selective progesterone receptor modulator that can be effective up to 120 hours post-intercourse. Both options provide critical options for women seeking to prevent unintended pregnancies, but their time frames and mechanisms of action differ, catering to varying needs and preferences in emergency contraception.</p></p>
