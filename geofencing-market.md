<p><p>The geofencing market is primarily divided into two types: fixed geofencing and mobile geofencing. Fixed geofencing refers to defined geographic boundaries established by stationary structures or locations, such as retail stores or events, to trigger alerts or marketing upon entry or exit. In contrast, mobile geofencing leverages GPS technology to create dynamic, flexible boundaries around users' movements, enabling real-time engagement with applications based on their location. Both types enhance targeted marketing and user interaction, optimizing customer experiences.</p></p>
