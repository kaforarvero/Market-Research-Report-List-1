<p><p>E-glass textile yarn is primarily categorized into two types: fiber spinning and fiber roving. Fiber spinning involves the process of drawing and twisting glass fibers into continuous yarns used for various applications, offering high tensile strength and durability. In contrast, fiber roving refers to a bundle of unspun glass fibers that can be woven or knitted into fabrics or used in composites. Both types serve critical roles in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and construction, providing lightweight, corrosion-resistant solutions.</p></p>
